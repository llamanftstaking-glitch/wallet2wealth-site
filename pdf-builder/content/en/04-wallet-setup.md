Before we set anything up, let's kill the biggest misconception in crypto.

<img class="content-image" src="{{IMG_05}}" alt="Wallet Concept Visual"/>

**A wallet doesn't store your crypto.** The blockchain stores your crypto. A wallet stores the private key — the proof that you own it. Think of it like this: your money isn't inside your debit card. It's in the bank. The card is just how you access it. Same idea here, except with a crypto wallet, there's no bank. You are the bank.

That distinction matters because it means: lose your wallet app, you're fine. Lose your private key (seed phrase), you lose everything.

<img class="content-image" src="{{IMG_06}}" alt="Bank vs Wallet Diagram"/>

**Hot wallets vs. cold wallets**

A hot wallet is software connected to the internet — an app on your phone or a browser extension on your computer. It's convenient, fast, and free to set up. MetaMask is the most widely used hot wallet in the world. Hot wallets are great for getting started and for amounts you're actively using. The tradeoff: anything connected to the internet has more attack surface. Keep only what you're willing to risk in a hot wallet.

<img class="content-image" src="{{IMG_08}}" alt="Hot vs Cold Wallet"/>

A cold wallet is a physical hardware device — like a Ledger or Trezor — that stores your private key offline. It never touches the internet unless you plug it in to approve a transaction. Cold wallets cost $50–$150 and are the gold standard for storing serious amounts of crypto. You don't need one to get started, but once you have more than a few hundred dollars in crypto, it's worth considering.

For now, we're starting with MetaMask. It's free, it's trusted, and it works.

**Setting up MetaMask — step by step**

Step 1: Go to metamask.io from your desktop browser. Only download from the official site. Don't search "MetaMask download" and click the first ad — that ad may be a phishing site.

Step 2: Click "Download" and install the browser extension for Chrome, Firefox, Brave, or Edge. MetaMask is a browser extension, not a standalone app on desktop.

Step 3: Open the extension and click "Create a new wallet." Agree to the terms.

Step 4: Create a strong password. This password unlocks MetaMask on your device only — it does not protect your funds if someone steals your seed phrase. Don't reuse a password from another site.

Step 5: MetaMask will show you your Secret Recovery Phrase — 12 random words. This is the most important moment of the whole setup. Stop what you're doing. Get a pen and paper.

Step 6: Write the 12 words down, in order, by hand. Double-check every word. Then write them again on a second piece of paper as a backup.

Step 7: Store those papers somewhere safe. Not in a note on your phone. Not in a Google Doc. Not in a photo on your camera roll. On paper, somewhere secure — a drawer, a safe, wherever you keep important documents.

Step 8: MetaMask will ask you to confirm the seed phrase by selecting words in the correct order. This is just a verification step. Complete it to finish setup.

Your wallet is now live. You'll see your wallet address — a long string starting with 0x. This is your public address. It's safe to share — it's how people send crypto to you.

**Seed phrase rules — non-negotiable**

<img class="content-image" src="{{IMG_07}}" alt="Seed Phrase Security"/>

Never store it digitally — no notes apps, no email drafts, no screenshots.

Never share it with anyone — not tech support, not a friend, not a family member.

Make at least one physical backup and keep it in a different location than the first.

If your seed phrase is ever compromised, move your funds to a new wallet immediately.

:::checklist
### Wallet Setup Checklist

- Downloaded MetaMask only from metamask.io (not a search ad)
- Created a strong, unique password for the extension
- Wrote seed phrase down by hand on paper — twice
- Confirmed every word is spelled correctly and in the right order
- Stored both copies in separate, secure physical locations
- Completed the seed phrase verification step inside MetaMask
- Copied your wallet address (0x...) somewhere handy for the next section
- Never typed seed phrase into any website or app
:::
