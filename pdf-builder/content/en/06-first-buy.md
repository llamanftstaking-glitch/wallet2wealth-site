## Where to Buy: Start With Coinbase

There are dozens of exchanges. For a beginner, use Coinbase.

It's regulated in the US. It carries FDIC insurance on the cash in your account. The interface was built for people who've never touched crypto. It's not the cheapest option — their fees are higher than competitors — but you're not optimizing for fees on your first $50. You're optimizing for not making a mistake.

When you're comfortable, you can explore Kraken or Gemini for lower fees. For now: Coinbase.

## Step-by-Step: Your First Purchase

**Step 1 — Create your account.**
Go to coinbase.com. Enter your email, create a strong password. Enable 2FA immediately using an authenticator app — not SMS.

**Step 2 — Verify your identity.**
Coinbase is a regulated financial institution. They're required by law to verify who you are. Upload a photo of your government ID and take a selfie. This usually takes 5–10 minutes.

**Step 3 — Add a payment method.**
Connect a bank account (cheapest, takes 3–5 days to clear) or a debit card (instant, slightly higher fee). For your first buy, a debit card is fine — you want the experience today, not in a week.

**Step 4 — Buy Bitcoin or Ethereum.**
Use the search bar. Type "Bitcoin" or "Ethereum." Hit Buy. Enter your amount. Confirm.

That's it. You're a crypto owner.

**Step 5 — Transfer to your wallet.**
Don't leave it on Coinbase. Copy your MetaMask wallet address. In Coinbase, go to Send and paste your wallet address. Send your crypto to your own wallet.

You just moved from "holding a promise" to "holding the real thing."

## Why Bitcoin or Ethereum — Not a Memecoin

Every week someone discovers a coin that's up 1,000% and thinks they missed it. They buy the top. It crashes 90%. They learn an expensive lesson.

Bitcoin is the most secure, most liquid, most battle-tested crypto asset in the world. 15 years old. Survived every crash. Still here.

Ethereum is the network that powers most of the applications in crypto — DeFi, NFTs, smart contracts. It has real usage and real developers building on it.

Memecoins have a hype cycle. Most go to zero. They are not investments for beginners. They're bets. Start with the foundations before you gamble.

## How Much to Start With

$25 to $100. No more.

That's enough to feel the experience — watching the price move, sending to a wallet, checking a blockchain explorer. It's not enough to do serious damage if something goes wrong.

You are buying an education, not trying to get rich on your first trade. Add more once you understand what you're doing.

## Market Order vs. Limit Order

**Market order**: Buy right now at the current price. What Coinbase uses by default.

**Limit order**: Buy only if the price drops to a specific number you set. Available on Coinbase Advanced.

Use a market order for your first buy. Keep it simple.

## After You Buy: Get It Off the Exchange

We said it in Section 3. We'll say it again here.

Leave crypto on an exchange and you don't own it. Send it to your MetaMask wallet. You now control the private key. That's actual ownership.

The transfer costs a small network fee — usually under $1 for Ethereum, pennies for other networks. Worth every cent.
