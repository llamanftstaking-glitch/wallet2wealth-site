## Glossary

You don't need to memorize these. Use this page as a reference when you encounter a term you don't recognize. The crypto space is full of jargon — some of it meaningful, some of it designed to sound more complex than it is. This list covers the terms that actually matter for a beginner building a safe, long-term strategy.

Bookmark this page. Come back to it whenever something doesn't click.

<table class="glossary-table">
<tr><th>Term</th><th>What It Means</th></tr>
<tr><td>Blockchain</td><td>A public, permanent record of transactions stored across thousands of computers. No single owner or controller.</td></tr>
<tr><td>Wallet</td><td>Software that holds your private keys and lets you manage your crypto. Your crypto lives on the blockchain — the wallet is your access key.</td></tr>
<tr><td>Seed Phrase</td><td>12–24 words that are the master key to your wallet. Anyone with these words owns your crypto. Never share. Never store digitally.</td></tr>
<tr><td>Private Key</td><td>A secret code that proves you own a wallet address. Your seed phrase generates this automatically.</td></tr>
<tr><td>Gas Fee</td><td>The cost to process a transaction on a blockchain network. Paid to the network validators, not to any company.</td></tr>
<tr><td>Exchange</td><td>A platform where you buy and sell crypto. Centralized (Coinbase, Kraken) or decentralized (Uniswap).</td></tr>
<tr><td>DCA</td><td>Dollar-Cost Averaging. Investing a fixed amount at regular intervals regardless of price. Reduces the impact of volatility.</td></tr>
<tr><td>Stablecoin</td><td>Crypto pegged to a stable value (usually $1 USD). Examples: USDC, USDT. Used as a tool, not an investment.</td></tr>
<tr><td>Altcoin</td><td>Any cryptocurrency that isn't Bitcoin. Ranges from legitimate projects to pure speculation.</td></tr>
<tr><td>Cold Wallet</td><td>A hardware device (like Ledger) that stores your private keys offline. Much more secure than a hot wallet for large holdings.</td></tr>
<tr><td>Hot Wallet</td><td>A software wallet connected to the internet (like MetaMask). Convenient for everyday use but more exposure to risk.</td></tr>
<tr><td>DeFi</td><td>Decentralized Finance. Financial apps (lending, trading, earning) built on blockchain without banks or middlemen.</td></tr>
<tr><td>Smart Contract</td><td>Self-executing code on a blockchain that runs automatically when conditions are met. No human needed in the middle.</td></tr>
<tr><td>Market Cap</td><td>Total value of a cryptocurrency: price × circulating supply. Higher market cap generally means more established and less volatile.</td></tr>
<tr><td>Bull Market</td><td>Period of rising prices and positive market sentiment. Usually brings FOMO and irrational buying.</td></tr>
<tr><td>Bear Market</td><td>Period of falling prices. Usually triggers fear and panic selling — often the best time to DCA.</td></tr>
<tr><td>Staking</td><td>Locking crypto to help secure a blockchain and earn rewards. Like savings interest, but with crypto price risk.</td></tr>
<tr><td>Rug Pull</td><td>A scam where project creators drain funds and disappear. Common with new, hyped tokens with anonymous teams.</td></tr>
</table>
