## The Emotions You Will Feel

<img class="content-image" src="{{IMG_24}}" alt="Market Emotion Wheel"/>

Nobody tells you about this part.

You can read every article, watch every tutorial, and still be completely unprepared for how it *feels* to watch your portfolio drop 20% in a weekend. Crypto is not just a financial experience — it's an emotional one. And the market is engineered to exploit that.

Every emotion you feel has a name. Knowing them in advance is your first defense.

**Fear during drops.** The price falls. Your stomach drops with it. You start refreshing your portfolio every five minutes. You read headlines that say it's over. You wonder if you made a terrible mistake. This is fear — and it's exactly what shakes out weak hands and creates buying opportunities for everyone who holds. The drop itself is almost never the emergency. Your reaction to it is where the real damage happens.

**FOMO during pumps.** A coin you ignored is up 300% this week. Everyone on social media seems to be getting rich except you. So you buy — right at the top, right before it reverses. Fear of Missing Out is one of the most expensive emotions in investing. It turns you into a buyer when you should be watching, and a seller when you should be holding.

**Greed during bull markets.** Everything is green. Your portfolio is up 50%. You start thinking about quitting your job. You take bigger risks. You stop following your strategy. Greed makes you feel invincible right before the correction that reminds you you're not.

**Panic selling during corrections.** The red candles keep coming. You tell yourself you'll sell now and buy back lower. You sell. The price bounces without you. You've locked in a loss and missed the recovery. Panic selling is the single most reliable way to buy high and sell low — the exact opposite of what you're trying to do.

:::quote
"The most expensive words in investing are 'I can't take this anymore.'"
:::

The market is engineered to shake out emotional investors. Every panic sell is someone else's discount buy. Price volatility isn't a glitch — it's the mechanism that transfers wealth from people who react to people who respond.

Your job isn't to feel nothing. Your job is to feel everything and act on none of it.

## Common Beginner Mistakes

Most money lost in crypto isn't lost to bad luck. It's lost to predictable, avoidable mistakes. Here are the ones that hit beginners hardest:

- **Buying random coins before understanding them.** If you can't explain in one sentence what a coin does and why it has value, you're speculating — not investing. "Someone on Reddit said it's the next Bitcoin" is not a thesis.

- **Leaving funds on exchanges long-term.** Exchanges get hacked. Exchanges freeze withdrawals. Exchanges go bankrupt (FTX lost billions in customer funds). Your crypto is not yours until it's in a wallet you control.

- **Checking your portfolio every hour.** Hourly price checks don't generate returns — they generate anxiety. Anxiety generates impulsive decisions. Impulsive decisions generate losses. Check your portfolio on a schedule, not on impulse.

- **Storing your seed phrase digitally.** Not in a screenshot. Not in your notes app. Not in Google Drive or iCloud. Not in a text message to yourself. Any digital storage is a potential attack surface. Your seed phrase belongs on paper, in a safe, offline.

- **Panic selling during normal corrections.** A 20–40% drawdown is a routine event in crypto, not a catastrophe. Selling at the bottom of a correction and buying back at a higher price is the most common self-inflicted wound in this space.

- **Overtrading trying to time the market.** Every trade costs fees. Every trade is a taxable event. Every trade requires you to be right twice — when to exit and when to re-enter. Most people who trade actively underperform people who simply hold.

- **Following influencer calls without research.** Influencers are often paid to promote tokens, or already holding and looking for others to pump the price. If they're telling you to buy, they may already be planning to sell.

- **Investing money you actually need.** Crypto can drop 80% and take years to recover. Never use rent money, emergency funds, or debt to invest in volatile assets. Only invest what you can afford to lose entirely.

## The W2W Rules

*Print this. Put it somewhere you'll see it.*

<ol class="rules-list">
<li data-num="01">Never invest money you cannot afford to lose completely</li>
<li data-num="02">Never share your seed phrase with anyone, ever</li>

</ol>

These aren't suggestions. They're the difference between people who build wealth slowly and people who lose money quickly.
