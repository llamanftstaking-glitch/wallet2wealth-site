Most crypto losses aren't random. They follow a pattern: excitement replaced careful thinking, and someone bought something they didn't understand.

The W2W Standard is a repeatable filter. Run every investment through it. Not some investments — every one. The discipline of applying it consistently is what separates people who build wealth from people who chase losses.

## The Standard: 7 Checkpoints

Think of this as a pre-flight checklist. You don't skip items because you're excited to get on the plane.

<div class="checklist">

### W2W Quality Checklist

- I can explain what this project does in two sentences
- The team is publicly identifiable and has shipped products before
- There is a whitepaper with technical substance, not just marketing
- On-chain data shows real users and real activity
- The token has a specific purpose beyond speculation
- I've read criticism of this project, not just praise
- I am prepared to lose this entire investment without changing my life

</div>

If any box is unchecked, don't buy. Wait until you can check every box, or move on.

## Tier System: Where to Put Your Money

Not all crypto is the same risk level. Here's how to think in tiers:

**Tier 1 — Foundation (lowest risk)**

Bitcoin and Ethereum. Maximum liquidity. Maximum track record. Still volatile by traditional standards, but the most tested assets in the space. This is where 80–90% of a beginner's crypto allocation should live.

**Tier 2 — Established Altcoins (medium risk)**

Projects with years of track record, billions in market cap, and verifiable usage. Examples: Solana, Chainlink, Avalanche. These carry more risk than Tier 1 and require individual research before investing.

**Tier 3 — Speculative (high risk)**

Smaller market caps, newer projects, unproven teams. Some will generate massive returns. Most will fail. Only money you can afford to lose entirely belongs here. Maximum 5–10% of your crypto portfolio.

**Never** — Memecoins, anonymous teams with no product, "guaranteed return" platforms, anything promoted primarily by influencers.

## The Position Size Rule

How much to put in any single investment:

| Portfolio Size | Max single position |
|---|---|
| Under $1,000 | 50% (keep it simple, 2 assets) |
| $1,000–$5,000 | 25% |
| $5,000–$20,000 | 15% |
| Over $20,000 | 10% |

Concentration risk is real. If any single asset going to zero would ruin your strategy, the position is too large.

## Time Horizon Matching

Match your investment to your actual time horizon:

- **Under 6 months:** Don't be in crypto. Keep it in a high-yield savings account.
- **6 months–2 years:** Tier 1 only. Stablecoins if needed for liquidity.
- **2–5 years:** Tier 1 primary, small Tier 2 allocation possible.
- **5+ years:** Full strategy with all tiers appropriate.

Crypto is not a short-term vehicle. If you might need the money within a year, don't invest it.

:::detective
**The "Will I Care in 5 Years" Test**

Before any investment, ask: if this project still exists in 5 years, will it matter? Does it solve a real problem that will still exist? Is the technology genuinely useful?

Bitcoin: yes — sound money and digital gold will matter in 5 years.
A memecoin named after a dog: no — novelty doesn't last.

Most investments that feel urgent won't matter in 5 years. Most investments worth making don't require urgency.
:::

:::action
Apply the W2W checklist to your current portfolio right now. For every asset you hold, go through each checkpoint. If you find something that fails multiple checks, make a note. You don't have to sell immediately — but you should have a plan.
:::
