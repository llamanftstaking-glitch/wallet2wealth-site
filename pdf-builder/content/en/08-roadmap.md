You've made your first move. Now let's talk about where this goes — not hype, not moonshot promises. A realistic, month-by-month plan that turns you from a beginner into someone who actually understands what they own and why.

---

## Month 1 — Foundation

This month is about getting the basics locked in. One decision at a time.

- **Wallet created and secured** — MetaMask set up, seed phrase written on paper and stored offline. Not in a screenshot. Not in your notes app.
- **First purchase made** — $25–$100 of BTC or ETH. Not to get rich. To get real. Once you've bought it, watched it move, and felt what that's like, crypto stops being abstract.
- **DCA plan set up** — Pick an amount you won't miss ($20–$50/week) and automate it. Coinbase and Kraken both support recurring buys. Set it and forget it.
- **1 hour/week of reading** — Start with CoinDesk and Decrypt. Skim headlines. You don't need to understand everything. Pattern recognition builds over time.

Month 1 goal: stop thinking about it and start doing it.

---

## Month 3 — Growing

By month 3, you have skin in the game. Now start developing real understanding.

- **Portfolio at $300–$500** — If you've been DCA'ing $50/week, you're right on track. You haven't tried to time the market. You've just stayed consistent.
- **Understand what you own** — Pick one asset in your portfolio and read its whitepaper or a plain-English summary. What problem does it solve? Who uses it? Why should it exist in 10 years?
- **Try staking for passive yield** — Ethereum and BNB both support staking. Staking means you lock your tokens to help validate the network and earn a small reward — typically 3–6% annually. Low risk, good education.
- **Join one community** — Find a Discord server or browse r/CryptoCurrency on Reddit. Lurk first. Ask one question. Start building context from people who've been doing this longer than you.

Month 3 goal: know what you own and why you own it.

---

## Month 6 — Informed

Six months in, you're no longer a beginner. You're someone who has stayed consistent through market noise. Now go deeper.

- **Review your portfolio allocation** — Are you 100% in one coin? Consider diversifying. Are you holding things you no longer believe in? It's okay to rebalance.
- **Consider a hardware wallet** — If your holdings have crossed $1,000, a Ledger hardware wallet is worth the $79 investment. Cold storage means your crypto isn't reachable online — and that matters.
- **Understand your tax obligations** — In most countries, crypto is taxed as property. Every trade is a taxable event. Use Koinly or CoinTracker to pull your transaction history into a readable report before tax season.
- **Set your long-term thesis** — Write down what you believe and why. Which projects do you think will matter in 5 years? What's your exit strategy — or are you planning to hold? Having a thesis means you make decisions instead of reactions.

Month 6 goal: you have a strategy, not just a position.

## Resources

These are the tools and sources worth bookmarking. No filler.

**Wallets**
- MetaMask — best hot wallet for beginners (browser extension + mobile)
- Ledger — best hardware wallet for cold storage

**Exchanges**
- Coinbase — cleanest onboarding, best for first-time buyers
- Kraken — lower fees, more assets, good for intermediate users

**News & Research**
- CoinDesk — reliable daily coverage
- Decrypt — beginner-friendly explainers
- The Block — institutional-grade reporting

**On-Chain Data**
- Glassnode — understand what big holders are doing
- CryptoQuant — exchange flows and market signals

**Taxes**
- Koinly — connects to exchanges and wallets, generates tax reports
- CoinTracker — solid alternative, good UI

**Community**
- wallet2wealth.com — where this journey continues
